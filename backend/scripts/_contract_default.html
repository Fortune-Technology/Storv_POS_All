3.5 Card Brand Rules & Compliance

Merchant agrees to comply at all times with the operating rules, regulations, and guidelines of all Card Brands. Card Brand violations may result in fines, penalties, or termination of processing privileges without notice. Merchant specifically agrees:

  • Not to impose a surcharge on card transactions except where expressly permitted by applicable law AND Card Brand rules.
  • Not to set a minimum or maximum transaction amount for card payments except as permitted by Card Brand rules.
  • Not to accept card payments for goods or services not disclosed in the Merchant application, or for cash advances.
  • Not to submit a transaction for a sale that was not completed in person or authorized by the cardholder.
  • Not to split a single transaction into multiple smaller transactions to avoid authorization thresholds.
  • To maintain PCI DSS compliance at all times and complete the required annual Self-Assessment Questionnaire.
  • Not to store full card numbers, CVV/CVC codes, or PIN data after transaction authorization.

3.6 Chargebacks & Disputes

Merchant is solely responsible for managing and responding to chargebacks. Failure to respond within the timeframe specified (typically 7–10 business days) may result in automatic loss of the disputed amount. StoreVeu reserves the right to establish a chargeback reserve account if Merchant's chargeback rate exceeds 1% of monthly transaction volume for two or more consecutive months.

Merchant must display the payment acceptance marks (Visa, Mastercard, Discover, Amex) at all points of sale where those cards are accepted, in accordance with each Card Brand's current logo usage guidelines.

4.3 Surcharging & Dual Pricing Advertising Rules

If enrolled in a Dual Pricing program, Merchant must display both the cash price and the card price clearly and equally prominently, post the required disclosure notice at the store entrance and every point of sale, and never describe the differential as a "fee," "surcharge," or "processing fee" in states where surcharging is prohibited (including Massachusetts).

Merchant shall not use the StoreVeu platform for any purpose other than the lawful operation of the business described in the Merchant application. Prohibited uses include processing transactions for third parties (factoring), probing systems, attempting to access other merchants' data, transmitting malicious code, circumventing security features, or scraping platform data.

Misuse of the platform may result in immediate suspension without notice, termination of this Agreement, and StoreVeu's pursuit of damages including attorney's fees and court costs.

Section 6 — Term & Termination

6.1 Term

This Agreement commences on the Effective Date and continues month-to-month following the Minimum Term of three (3) months, unless earlier terminated.

6.2 Termination by Merchant

After the Minimum Term, Merchant may terminate by submitting written notice to support@storeveu.com on or before the 15th of any calendar month. Termination is effective at month-end. Notice received after the 15th results in one additional month of charges.

6.3 Termination by StoreVeu

StoreVeu may terminate or suspend services immediately without notice upon:

  • Merchant's breach not cured within 5 business days of written notice.
  • Chargeback ratio exceeding 1% for two consecutive months.
  • Fraudulent activity, money laundering, or illegal use of the platform.
  • Violation of Card Brand rules.
  • Direction from acquiring bank, Card Brand, or law enforcement.
  • Insolvency, bankruptcy filing, or assignment for benefit of creditors.
  • False or misleading information provided.
  • Non-payment of any fee for more than 10 business days after due date.

StoreVeu may also terminate for any reason upon 30 days' written notice.

6.4 Effect of Termination

Upon termination: (a) Merchant's license is immediately revoked; (b) all outstanding fees become immediately due; (c) pinpad/EMV reader devices must be returned within 14 days or a $150.00 per-device replacement charge applies; (d) StoreVeu provides a final statement within 30 days; (e) transaction data is retained for 18 months for legal/compliance purposes; (f) Merchant data exports must be requested before the termination date.

6.5 Survival

Sections relating to payment of fees, intellectual property, indemnification, limitation of liability, dispute resolution, and confidentiality survive termination indefinitely.
